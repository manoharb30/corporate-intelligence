## Outcomes

## Interpretation

The midcap filter ($300M–$5B) isn't arbitrary — it's load-bearing. Small-cap and large-cap insider buying DOES happen (78 and 87 candidate clusters respectively in 22 months), and individual winners exist, but the aggregate hit rate in those size bands is ~12 percentage points lower than midcap. That's substantive, but the Bonferroni correction says we don't have sample size to distinguish "systematic 12pp gap" from "variance within the same distribution" at the p<0.05 bar.

The combined pool hit raw p=0.053 — close enough that with another ~200 signals of similar quality, we'd likely cross the significance threshold.
